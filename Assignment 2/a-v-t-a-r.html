Visual effects

Cameron pioneered a specially designed camera built into a 6-inch boom that allowed the facial expressions of the actors to be captured and digitally recorded for the animators to use later. [76]

A number of innovative  visual effects  techniques were used during production. According to Cameron, work on the film had been delayed since the 1990s to allow the techniques to reach the necessary degree of advancement to adequately portray his vision of the film. [13] [14]  The director planned to make use of photorealistic computer-generated characters, created using new  motion capture  animation technologies he had been developing in the 14 months leading up to December 2006
